**Day 11 [May 31, Sun] — Apple iPhone**

In 2007, Steve Jobs introduced three products: a wide-screen iPod with touch controls, a revolutionary mobile phone, and a breakthrough internet communications device.

They weren't three separate devices. They were one.

Four decisions that changed the world:
→ Betting on Multitouch. Apple bought FingerWorks in 2005 because Jobs realized the stylus was a dead end. 
→ The "Desktop-Class" OS. Running a variant of OS X meant the iPhone could do what "WAP browsers" couldn't.
→ Gorilla Glass. Scratches on plastic prototypes led to a frantic 6-month partnership with Corning to mass-produce glass that didn't exist yet.
→ No Keyboard. It was the ultimate "soft" bet. You traded tactile feel for a screen that could be anything.
