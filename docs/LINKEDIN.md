**Day 21 — Netflix**

Netflix killed its own DVD business while it was still profitable. Most companies wait until they're forced.

Reed Hastings announced the streaming pivot in 2007, when DVD revenue was $1.2B and growing. The streaming product was barely usable.

Four bets that defined modern media:

→ Cannibalizing yourself before competitors do. By the time Blockbuster understood streaming, Netflix was 5 years ahead.
→ The Qwikster split was a $1B mistake, but the strategic instinct (separate DVD and streaming) was right. Execution failed; the thesis didn't.
→ Original content (House of Cards, 2013) was the second pivot. Acquired content costs were going to rise indefinitely.
→ Algorithmic personalization over human curation. Cinematch beat Blockbuster's editorial bets every quarter.
